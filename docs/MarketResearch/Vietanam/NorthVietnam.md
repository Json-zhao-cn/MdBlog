#### 1. ***Geographic postion***

Vietnam is located in the eastern part of the Indochinese Peninsula in Asia, and its geographical location is of great significance. 
1. **Latitude and longitude**: Vietnam is situated between 8°27′N and 23°23′N latitude and 102°10′E and 109°30′E longitude, and it belongs to the Eastern Time Zone 7.
2. **Maritime and land position**: Vietnam borders the South China Sea to the east and the Gulf of Thailand to the south, with a coastline of over 3,260 kilometers. Its northern part is adjacent to the Gulf of Tonkin, which is rich in marine resources and an important maritime transportation route.
3. **Relative position**: Vietnam shares a border with China to the north and is bordered by Laos and Cambodia to the west. It serves as a vital link between Southeast Asia and East Asia and holds a significant strategic position in regional economic cooperation and trade.
4. **Topographic position**: Vietnam has a long and narrow shape, slightly resembling an "S". It is about 1,650 kilometers from north to south and only 50 kilometers at its narrowest point from east to west. The terrain is high in the northwest and low in the southeast. Three quarters of the country is mountainous and highland, while only a quarter is plain. The main plains are the Red River Delta and the Mekong Delta.
